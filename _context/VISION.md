# Vision

A React app for busy parents built around three real daily pain points: what do we do, what is that, and what do we eat. Powered by the Anthropic Claude API.

## The problem

Parenting a toddler involves a constant stream of small but exhausting decisions. This app handles three of the most common ones so parents can spend less time thinking and more time present with their kid.

## The three pillars

### 1. Activity Generator
Parents input their situation — kid's age range, energy level, time available, indoor or outdoor, what supplies are around — and get tailored activity ideas with simple instructions. No more blank-staring at a bored toddler.

### 2. World Explorer (What is THAT?)
Parents take or upload a photo of something their toddler is curious about. The app identifies it, explains it in age-appropriate language, turns it into a short story, and gives follow-up questions to spark curiosity. Age range is parent-selected so the explanation fits the kid.

### 3. Kid-Friendly Recipes
Parents describe what's in the fridge or pantry and the app suggests quick, practical meals that work for toddlers — with notes on how to make each one toddler-friendly.

## Who it's for

Busy parents of toddlers (roughly ages 1–5) who want a fast, useful tool — not a parenting blog or a generic AI chatbot.

## The bigger picture

This project is also a learning exercise in applied AI development — specifically: dynamic prompt construction, structured output from Claude, multimodal input (image + text), and multi-step agentic thinking. Each phase teaches something new about how AI systems work under the hood.
